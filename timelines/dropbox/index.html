Dropbox
01 June, 2007
Drew Houston began making something for his personal use, but then realized that it could benefit others with the same problems. Houston founded Dropbox, Inc. in June 2007, and shortly thereafter secured seed funding from Y Combinator.
08 September, 2008
Dropbox officially launched at 2008's TechCrunch50, an annual technology conference by MIT students Drew Houston and Arash Ferdowsi. Owing to trademark disputes between Proxy, Inc. and Evenflow (Dropbox's parent company), Dropbox's official domain name was "getdropbox.com".
08 October, 2009
Company acquires its current domain, "dropbox.com".
01 May, 2010
Dropbox users in China were unable to access Dropbox. Later, Dropbox confirmed they had been blocked by the Chinese government.
01 May, 2011
Dropbox struck deals with Japanese mobile service providers Softbank and Sony Ericsson. As per the terms of the deal Dropbox will come preloaded on their mobile telephones.
01 April, 2012
Dropbox announces a new feature allowing users to automatically upload photographs or videos from camera, tablet, SD card, or smartphone.
26 September, 2012
Facebook and Dropbox integrated to allow group users to share files to Facebook Groups using Dropbox’s cloud-based storage system. The feature allows users to directly share inside Facebook's group pages without exiting the Facebook domain.
12 November, 2012
Dropbox announces it had reached 100 million users.
19 December, 2012
Dropbox acquires Snapjoy, which provides a service for aggregating, archiving and viewing all digital photographs taken with cameras, phones, or popular photo applications.
01 February, 2013
Dropbox was responsible for 0.29% of all worldwide Internet traffic.
15 March, 2013
Dropbox acquires the email management application for iOS Mailbox.
20 July, 2013
Dropbox acquires mobile coupon startup Endorse.
13 November, 2013
Dropbox announces it had reached 200 million users, and announced changes to "Dropbox for Business".
21 November, 2013
Dropbox releases new application versions for iOS. The new design has a cleaner and simpler user interface which brings the app in-line with other iOS 7 offerings.
01 February, 2014
Dropbox is unblocked in China, although the reason for this is still unclear
09 April, 2014
Dropbox announced that Condoleezza Rice would be joining their board of directors, prompting protests from some users who were concerned about her appointment.
17 April, 2014
Dropbox acquires Hackpad, a real-time collaborative text editor.
21 May, 2014
Dropbox announces that it had bought 3D Photo Sharing App Bubbli. Bubbli's founders Ben Newhouse and Terrence McArdle said they were "thrilled" to be joining Dropbox.
01 June, 2014
Dropbox is blocked again, using DNS spoofing.
16 September, 2014
Dropbox discoveres that Apple’s new iOS 8 introduces a compatibility issue that may prevent Dropbox and Carousel from properly uploading a user's photos and videos.
01 November, 2014
Dropbox announces a partnership with Microsoft to integrate Dropbox and Microsoft Office applications on iOS, Android and the Office 365 applications on the web.
21 January, 2015
Dropbox acquires CloudOn, a company that provided mobile applications for document editing and creation.
01 July, 2015
Dropbox announces the acquisition of Clementine, an enterprise communication service.
