Uber
share with: 
01 March, 2009
Uber was founded as "UberCab" by Travis Kalanick and Garrett Camp in 2009 and the app.
01 July, 2010
Uber goes live for the first time in San Francisco.
01 December, 2010
Ryan Graves steps down as CEO in favor of Travis Kalanick.
01 February, 2011
Uber raises $11 million in Series A round led by Benchmark Capital.
01 May, 2011
Uber goes live in New York City.
01 December, 2011
Uber raises $37 million in Series B round, including investors such as Goldman Sachs, Menlo Ventures, and Bezos Expeditions.
01 December, 2011
Uber expands beyond the United States, starting by expanding into Paris, France.
01 July, 2012
Uber announces UberX, a service that uses lower-cost hybrid vehicles.
01 July, 2012
Uber launches in London, United Kingdom.
01 August, 2012
Lyft, a competitor to Uber, launches in San Francisco.
01 August, 2013
Uber raises $258 million in Series C round, with investors including Google Ventures.
01 August, 2013
Uber expands to India, launching its first product in Bangalore.
01 August, 2013
Uber expands to Africa, launches its first product in Johannesburg, South Africa.
01 April, 2014
Uber launches Uber Rush in New York City, a courier service using bicycle messengers to deliver packages. This marks the beginning of Uber's transition into a logistics company.
01 June, 2014
Uber raises $1.2 billion in a Series D round, with a valuation of over $18 billion.
01 July, 2014
Uber officially launches in China, starting with Beijing.
01 July, 2014
Uber officially launches in Lagos, Nigeria, expanding its presence to Western Africa.
01 August, 2014
Uber announces UberPool, which lets riders share rides based on proximity.
01 August, 2014
Uber announces a "test" for a Corner Store, which allows customers to order basic items.
01 December, 2014
Uber raises $600 million in Series E round from Baidu.
01 January, 2015
Uber launches its first product in East Africa, in Nairobi, Kenya.
01 January, 2015
Uber launches Uber Cargo, which allows people to transport packages via Uber drivers. This starts in Hong Kong, and marks the transition of Uber into a logistics company.
01 February, 2015
Uber opens robotics research facility In Pittsburgh to build self-driving cars. In May, Uber poaches 50 employees from Carnegie Mellon's National Robotics Engineering Center.
01 June, 2015
Uber makes its first public acquisition, a map startup called deCarta.
01 June, 2015
California Labor Commission deems Uber drivers as employees.
01 July, 2015
Administrative judge recommends that Uber be fined $7.3 million and suspended from operating in California.
