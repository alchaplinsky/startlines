Booking
01 September, 1996
Bookings.nl is founded by Geert-Jan Bruinsma in Amsterdam advertising hotels in Amsterdam and later in the rest of the Netherlands. Booking.com is an online booking website started as a small start-up in Enschede. Booking.com B.V. is registered with the trade register of the Chamber of Commerce in Amsterdam, the Netherlands, under registration number 31047344. VAT registration number is NL805734958B01. Registration number with Dutch Data Protection Authority is 1288246.
01 January, 1997
The first 1st hotel was presented on the site. Later in 2005 there were more than 10000 hotels.
01 January, 1999
Geert-Jan hires his first employee. It took him three months to decide to hire and he still sees it as one of the biggest steps the company ever made.
01 January, 2000
There were 395.555 accommodations online, 41 language, more than 115 offices worldwide, 57100 global destinations, 550000 room nights booked, 6000 employees worldwide every day.
01 January, 2002
For the first time, the 6-year-old company expanded outside of the Netherlands. The first new markets: London, Paris, and Barcelona. Within a year, these 3 cities were as successful as the entire country of the Netherlands had been.
01 October, 2003
Barcelona office opened. And till 2008 Booking.com represented over 5,400 Spanish hotels.
01 July, 2005
The Priceline Group acquired European hotel booking website Booking.com for a mere $133 million, and paired it with Cambridge, UK-headquartered Active Hotels. The latter had been acquired about 10 months Booking.com and Active Hotels eschewed the merchant model in favor of a retail, or agency, model and replaced complexity with simplicity and efficiency for hotels.earlier for $161 million. Priceline is founded 8 years earlier in 1997 in Norwalk.
27 November, 2006
Priceline.com-owned online hotel booking firm Active Hotels is changing its name to Booking.com. The name change is designed to enable the company to operate under a common business model on a pan-European basis.
31 December, 2007
Booking.com has opened its offices in New York and Singapore. Later, in April 2008 Booking.com has opened two new offices in New York and San Francisco as further commitment to its business expansion in the United States and North American Region.
25 August, 2008
Booking.com has opened a second office in Spain, bringing the total number of branches to 21 worldwide. Booking.com said that the expansion is intended to broaden its market reach and better serve its local hotel supplier/partners.
31 December, 2009
Booking.com expands and San Paulo office was opened. Also in this year there were more than 2000 employees.
01 April, 2010
The iPhone apps was launched. Booking and reservation management features included searching, comparing and booking at IHG properties, planning arrival by accessing current reservations and quick access to maps, directions and hotel contact information, securely storing credit card information to expedite bookings.
01 November, 2010
Booking.com launched an iPad version and later in February 2011 an Android version and more recently, a Windows 8 app for its tablets. In its last results call, the company reported 10 million installs of the various applications worldwide.
31 December, 2010
Booking.com expands to 35 offices and 100000 hotels.
01 September, 2011
Darren Huston, a former Microsoft and Starbucks executive became Booking.com's CEO
01 September, 2011
Darren Huston, was appointed as Chief Executive Officer of booking.com in September 2011 by the Priceline Group and has served as President and Chief Executive Officer of The Priceline Group since January 1, 2014.
10 April, 2012
Booking.com announced the launch of the first global last-minute hotel app, ‘Booking.com Tonight’. It is an app designed for iPhone and iPod touch.
31 July, 2012
The United Kingdom's Office of Fair Trading found that Booking.com may have been limiting price competition on hotel room sales. Booking.com does not charge a booking fee to customers, but charges commission on bookings to accommodation partners.
07 August, 2012
CTRIP.com International Ltd (Ctrip), a Chinese online travel company, and Booking.com formed a partnership with a commercial agreement. This allows Ctrip to access Booking.com's global portfolio.
16 October, 2012
Booking.com announces today that the latest release of its iPhone app has a new feature: Passbook. Passbook allows users to easily access their hotel and accommodation bookings, along with entry tickets, boarding passes and much more all in one place. Adding Passbook to the app builds upon the seamless multi-device experience Booking.com strives to deliver to its growing base of over 10 million iPhone and iPod touch users.
29 October, 2012
Booking.com launched its first app in Windows version, which allows customers to complete their hotel and accommodation bookings directly from the new Windows 8 platform.
06 December, 2012
Booking.com announced the launch of its free native Kindle Fire app in all Amazon’s Appstores. The new app allows Booking.com’s customers to easily make and access their hotel and accommodation bookings anywhere, anytime via their Kindle Fire devices. The free Booking.com Kindle Fire app is available for download in all Amazon Appstores including the U.S., U.K., Germany, Spain, France, Italy and Japan, and is available for Kindle Fire 7”, Kindle Fire HD 7”, and Kindle Fire HD 8.9” devices. Booking.com consistently seeks to deliver innovation to its tens of millions of worldwide mobile customers, and is one of the first apps to integrate with the Amazon Maps API. The native Kindle Fire app is the latest addition to Booking.com’s already popular apps for the Apple, Android, and Windows 8 platforms.
31 December, 2012
Booking.com expands to 70 offices, more than 4000 employees, 250000 hotels.
22 January, 2013
Booking.com’s first brand campaign, ‘Booking.yeah’, was launched online, aired on television stations and in movie theaters and on TV networks, for the U.S. market with advertising agency Wieden + Kennedy Amsterdam.
20 February, 2013
Panorama Group, Indonesia’s largest tour and travel company, has spent $2 millions on launching bookpanorama.com. Strategic partnership have been formed with Booking.com, so that it will be able to access booking.com’s portfolio of more than 270,000 hotels across 179 countries in the world.
16 September, 2013
Australian online hotel booking service Booking.com has unveiled its major brand launch across the country via Wieden+Kennedy, Amsterdam. The campaign re-introduces Aussie travellers to a globally successful booking experience.
22 January, 2014
Booking.com launches first Canadian brand campaign. Canada is one of the world’s most robust travel markets, which is why Booking.com, the world’s No. 1 accommodation site, is making it a priority market.
01 February, 2014
booking.com announced that its mobile bookings grow 160% in 2013, which the total transaction value of mobile accommodation bookings grew over to $8 billion. In 2011, the mobile bookings’ figure was $1 billion and tripled to over $3 billion in 2012.
17 September, 2014
Sprylogics International Corp. a technology provider of local mobile search and messaging solutions for consumers and businesses has signed an affiliate agreement with Booking.com to integrate Booking.com's extensive hotel data into Sprylogics' Poynt App and Poynt-Enabled SDK. Booking.com has detailed information on over 500,000 hotels and other accommodation properties in 205 countries and includes over 65,000 unique destinations. In addition, Poynt users will be able access over 28 million guest reviews when searching to find and book the best accommodations available all without leaving the App. Features include last minute, secret and value deals, details of amenities, and the world's biggest data base of hotels, apartments, hostels, B&B's, campsites and more. Users will be able to complete fast and secure bookings directly from their Smartphone.
06 October, 2014
Ural Airlines has begun working with Booking.com. Because of this partnership, participants in the Wings program get up to 15% of the price of hotel bookings made on booking.com credited to their bonus accounts.
19 October, 2014
Booking.com was the top spender in the travel & tourism category for Google Adwords in 2011, with its estimated annual spending on Google Adwords of $40.4 million.
31 December, 2014
Booking.com expands to 150 offices, more than 8000 employees, 500000 hotels.
01 January, 2015
Booking.com B.V. is based in Amsterdam in the Netherlands, and is supported internationally by 170 offices in over 60 countries around the world.
01 March, 2015
Over 10000 people right across the world are dedicated to serving Booking.com’s customers and property-owning partners. With 226,100 hotels in the fold and counting, Booking.com has been experiencing phenomenal growth and kicking butt.
21 April, 2015
In April 2015 the French, Swedish and Italian competition authorities accepted a proposal by Booking.com to drop its "rate parity" clause and thereby allow competitor travel agents to offer lower hotel prices than Booking.com. Booking.com further agreed to extend and apply its proposal across all EU states. Hotels are still prevented from discounting prices directly on their own websites.
01 June, 2015
Each day, over 900,000 room nights are reserved on Booking.com. The Booking.com website and apps attract visitors from both the leisure and business sectors worldwide.
01 September, 2015
Booking.com have over 700,000 properties globally under contract. More than 4.5 million room nights are booked on the website every week. Booking.com provides a cost-effective commission-based model, a network of over 10000 affiliate partner websites, and a personalised account management service to help to optimise customer's revenue.
