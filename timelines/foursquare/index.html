Foursquare
23 March, 2009
The service was created in late 2008 and launched in 2009 on March 23 by Dennis Crowley and Naveen Selvadurai.
01 January, 2010
Foursquare changes their location model to allow check-ins from any location worldwide.
01 September, 2010
Foursquare announces version 2.0 of its check-in app which would help to direct users to new locations and activities, rather than just sharing their location with friends.
21 February, 2011
Foursquare reaches 7 million users IDs.
08 March, 2011
Foursquare 3.0 is released for Android and iPhone users.
04 July, 2011
An official Foursquare application for Windows Phone 7 also has all features.
08 August, 2011
President Barack Obama joins Foursquare, with the intention that the staff at the White House would use the service to post tips from places the president has visited.
12 October, 2011
Foursquare 4.0 was released for the iPhone, with other platforms following after. The update added a new feature called "Radar", powered by Foursquare's "Explore" algorithm.
01 March, 2012
Foursquare announces they would no longer be using Google Maps for their platform, instead moving to OpenStreetMap, via MapBox rendering and style. Foursquare stated they were happier to promote crowd-sourced, open data.
07 June, 2012
Foursquare launches a major redesign, which they described as a "whole new app". The app's "explore" function will now allow users to browse locations by category or conduct a specific search like "free wi-fi" or "dumplings"
05 November, 2012
Foursquare announces an update to their iOS mobile app that will showcase a ratings system to locations on a one to ten scale. The ratings will be based on tips, dislikes and ratings from previous check-ins.
07 November, 2012
Foursquare announces another update to their iOS mobile app, allowing users to check in to a location with Facebook friends who do not use the service themselves.
21 November, 2012
Foursquare announces a Recently Opened feature to its iOS app, allowing users to find recently opened locations in their areas.
10 April, 2013
Release of Foursquare 6.0
05 December, 2013
Release of Foursquare 7.0
04 February, 2014
Microsoft announced a partnership with Foursquare, giving access to their users’ physical movements and preferences. This data can be used to personalize search results on the search engine Bing.
05 May, 2014
Foursquare 8.0 and Swarm 1.0 - Splitting the Service. Users can not chek-in within Foursquare anymore, this functionality is moved to Swarm.
14 February, 2015
Foursquare’s Latest Update Makes It Officially A City Guide.
16 March, 2015
Foursquare has launched Pinpoint, a new advertising platform that takes advantage of its 7 billion check-ins and 55 million worldwide users to pull together its data.
02 June, 2015
Starting today, users can book an Uber straight from the Foursquare app.
23 June, 2015
Mayorships feature is added to swarm, as it formely was in Foursquare.
