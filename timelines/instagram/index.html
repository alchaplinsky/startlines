Instagram
06 October, 2010
Instagram launches. The name "Instagram" is a portmanteau of "instant camera" and "telegram".
12 December, 2010
In just two month Instagram gains 1 million users.
01 January, 2011
Instagram added hashtags to help users discover both photographs and each other. Instagram encourages users to make tags both specific and relevant, rather than tagging generic words like "photo," to make photographs stand out and to attract like-minded Instagram users.
02 February, 2011
An announcement revealed that Instagram had raised US$7 million in Series A funding from a variety of investors, including Benchmark Capital, Jack Dorsey, Chris Sacca (through Capital fund), and Adam D'Angelo. The deal valued Instagram at around $25 million.
21 March, 2011
First worldwide InstaMeet.
03 August, 2011
150 millionth Photo Upload.
20 September, 2011
Instagram introduces version 2.0 with 4 new filters: Amaro, Rise, Hudson, Valencia. Release also included high resolution photographs, optional borders, one click rotation, and an updated icon.
26 September, 2011
Instagram has 10 million users.
21 November, 2011
Launch of Weekend HashTag project.
09 December, 2011
Apple Inc. named Instagram "App of the Year" for 2011.
11 February, 2012
Instagram introduces version 2.1 with new "Lux" filter and improved look.
03 April, 2012
Instagram is released for Android phones running the 2.2 Froyo version of the OS, and it was downloaded more than one million times in less than one day.
09 April, 2012
Facebook made an offer to purchase Instagram, along with its 13 employees, for approximately US$1 billion in cash and stock, with a plan to keep the company independently managed.
28 June, 2012
Instagram redesigns photo pages for web.
26 July, 2012
Instagram has 80 million users.
16 August, 2012
Launch of Photo Maps
13 September, 2012
New mobile profile pages
05 November, 2012
Instagram rolls out profile for web
11 December, 2012
Instagram releases version 3.2 with new Willow filter and improved camera
18 December, 2012
Instagram updated its Terms of Service, granting itself the right—starting on January 16, 2013—to sell users' photos to third parties without notification or compensation.
21 December, 2012
Version 3.4.1 bring in 25 languages to the app.
05 February, 2013
Web feed rolls out.
26 February, 2013
Instagram announced 100 million active users, only two-and-a-half years after the launch of the app.
20 June, 2013
Initially a purely photo-sharing service, Instagram incorporated video sharing, allowing its users to record and share videos lasting for up to 15 seconds.
07 August, 2013
Announcing version 4.1
06 September, 2013
Company announces a total of more than 150 million monthly active users.
25 September, 2013
Instagram updates for iOS7.
24 October, 2013
Instagram introduced sponsored post advertising targeting US users in November 2013, and UK users in September 2014.
12 December, 2013
Instagram added Direct, a feature that allows users to send photos to specific people directly from the app. Instagram's primary intention with the Direct feature is to compete against messaging services, including Snapchat.
11 March, 2014
Instagram released an updated Android app with performance improvements and a flatter interface. The update was primarily intended to reduce the app's file size and resource usage.
15 August, 2014
Facebook's former Regional Director James Quarles was assigned the role, which manages Instagram’s revenue strategy, in addition to both the marketing and sales teams.
01 December, 2014
Instagram co-founder Kevin Systrom announced that Instagram has 300 million users accessing the site per month.
27 April, 2015
Three New Filters and Emoji hashtags added.
23 June, 2015
The All-New Search and Explore.
