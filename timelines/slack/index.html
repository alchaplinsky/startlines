Slack
01 August, 2013
Slack was founded by Stewart Butterfield ( with Eric Costello, Cal Henderson, and Serguei Mourachov) and launched in August 2013, and signed up 8000 customers within 24 hours of launch.
01 April, 2014
The company originally raised nearly $43 million.
01 October, 2014
The company raises $120 million in venture capital with a $1.2 billion valuation led by Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers and Google Ventures.
01 January, 2015
Slack announced the acquisition of Screenhero, which will be integrated into the Slack software to add voice, video, and screen sharing.
01 February, 2015
Company declares that it was adding around 10,000 new daily active users each week, and had more than 135,000 paying customers spread across 60,000 teams.
01 March, 2015
Slack signs a deal with investors to raise up to $160 million in a funding round that values the company at $2.76 billion. New investors include Institutional Venture Partners, Horizons Ventures, Index Ventures and DST Global.
18 March, 2015
Slack provides apps for Mac, iOS, and Android, and released an official Windows client.
01 April, 2015
The company raises another $160 million.
01 July, 2015
Slack adds Emoji Reactions, giving users the ability to add emoji buttons to their messages, which other users can then click on to express their reactions to messages.
