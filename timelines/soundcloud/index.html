Soundcloud
01 August, 2007
SoundCloud was originally founded in Stockholm, Sweden, but was established in Berlin in August 2007 by Swedish sound designer Alexander Ljung and Swedish artist Eric Wahlforss.
01 October, 2007
SoundCloud began to challenge the dominance of Myspace as a platform for musicians to distribute their music by allowing recording artists to interact more nimbly with their fans.

01 May, 2009

01 January, 2011
It was confirmed that SoundCloud had raised a US$10 million Series B funding round from Union Square Ventures and Index Ventures.
01 June, 2011
SoundCloud announced they had five million registered users, as well as investments from Ashton Kutcher and Guy Oseary's A-Grade Fund.
23 January, 2012
SoundCloud announced on their blog that they had reached 10 million registered users.
01 May, 2012
15 million users were announced by the company at a press conference held in San Francisco, US, where the next version of the site was previewed.
01 December, 2012
The new SoundCloud version was released to the general public. Heavily influenced by the tablet browsing experience and mobile devices.
01 March, 2013
Twitter announced SoundCloud as a third-party music partner, alongside iTunes, in developing the Twitter's first-ever integrated music app.
01 July, 2013
SoundCloud's registered users had quadrupled in number from the beginning of the previous year, with a total of 40 million, and an additional 20 million listeners were using the service on a monthly basis.
01 January, 2014
SoundCloud announced that it had commenced negotiations with the major music companies, as copyrighted material consistently appeared on the platform.
01 March, 2014
SoundCloud was reportedly in a second round of talks with major music labels about licensing after the January negotiations did not amount to anything substantial.
01 May, 2014
SoundCloud announced in an email to registered users that the Classic SoundCloud interface would be discontinued by the end of the month.
01 June, 2015
Soundcloud announced that it had struck a deal with over 20,000 independent labels to cut them in on royalties.
