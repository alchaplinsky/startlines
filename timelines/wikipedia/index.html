01 March, 2000
The Nupedia project was started. Its articles were written by volunteer contributors. It was founded by Jimmy Wales and underwritten by Bomis, with Larry Sanger as editor-in-chief. It is mostly known now as the predecessor of Wikipedia.
01 January, 2001
Wikipedia began as a side-project of Nupedia. Nupedia was founded upon the use of highly qualified volunteer contributors and an elaborate multi-step peer review process.
01 January, 2001
The license for Nupedia was changed to GFDL. The GNU Free Documentation License is a free content license for software documentation.
05 January, 2001
The idea of a wiki-based complement appeared during theconversation between Larry M. Sanger and Ben Kovitz.
11 January, 2001
The name was suggested by Sanger. He saw Wikipedia as a a project to produce a free content encyclopedia that can be edited by anyone.
12 January, 2001
The wikipedia.com domain name was registered . There was considerable resistance on the part of Nupedia's editors and reviewers to the idea of associating Nupedia with a wiki-style website, soSanger suggested giving the new project its own name, Wikipedia. The next day The wikipedia.org domain name was registered.
15 January, 2001
Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger launched Wikipedia. However, its technological and conceptual underpinnings predate this. The earliest known proposal for an online encyclopedia was made by Rick Gates in 1993.
16 January, 2001
The oldest article was created. Before this Wales made Wikipedia's first edit, a test edit with the text "Hello, World!".
17 January, 2001
The existence of the project was formally announced and an appeal for volunteers to engage in content creation was made to the Nupedia mailing list.
25 January, 2001
The first portable MediaWiki software went live on. MediaWiki is a free and open-source wiki application. It was developed by the Wikimedia Foundation and runs on many websites, including Wikipedia, Wiktionary and Wikimedia Commons. It is written in the PHP programming language and uses a backend database.

Using a relatively unknown and simple software tool called Wiki, they are involved in a kind of virtual barn-raising.
30 October, 2001
The content of the Wikipedia was released under an open license, the GNU FDL. As of July 15, 2009, Wikipedia has moved to a dual-licensing system that supersedes the previous GFDL only licensing. In short, this means that text licensed under the GFDL only can no longer be imported to Wikipedia, retroactive to November 1, 2008.
